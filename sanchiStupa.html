Sanchi Stupa , Madhaya Pradesh

Surrounded by the ruins of smaller stupas and monasteries, Sanchi Stupa is an architectural marvel in Sanchi, believed to house the relics of Lord Buddha. Also known as the Great Stupa, this famous pilgrimage site in India was built by the Indian Emperor Ashoka, the Great in the 3rd century BC.

As the oldest stone sculpture of the country, this UNESCO World Heritage Site takes you back in the days of the prosperous Mauryan Era. There are four intricately designed gateways surrounding the Stupa known as toranas, each individually symbolizing the four emotions of love, peace, courage and trust.

History

The Sanchi Stupa, located in Madhya Pradesh, India, is one of the oldest and most well-preserved Buddhist monuments in the country. It was originally commissioned by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE to house the relics of Lord Buddha. The stupa was later expanded and embellished during the Shunga and Satavahana dynasties (2ndâ€“1st century BCE), who added the intricately carved toranas (gateways) and a larger dome.

The Sanchi Stupa became an important center of Buddhist learning and pilgrimage, but its significance declined after the decline of Buddhism in India. The site remained largely abandoned until it was rediscovered by British officer General Taylor in 1818. Today, it is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and stands as a testament to ancient Buddhist art and architecture.

How to reach

By Air

  • The nearest airport is Raja Bhoj Airport, Bhopal (BHO), about 55 km from Sanchi.
  • From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Sanchi.

By Train

  • The nearest railway station is Sanchi Railway Station (SCI), about 1 km from the Stupa.
  • However, major trains stop at Bhopal Junction (BPL), which is 48 km away. From there, you can take a cab or local train to Sanchi.

By Road

  • From Bhopal: Sanchi is about 50 km away via NH146. You can take a bus, taxi, or drive yourself.
  • From Vidisha: It is just 10 km away, and auto-rickshaws or taxis are available.
  • From Indore: Sanchi is around 230 km away, and you can reach it by road via Bhopal.
